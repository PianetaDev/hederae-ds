EPOS ERIC: New Supercomputing Nodes Accelerate Climate Modelling Research
The latest expansion brings three new computing hubs online, reducing processing time by up to 60% for seismological and climate datasets.
EF
Elena Ferretti
22 marzo 2026
6 min
The European Plate Observing System (EPOS ERIC) announced this week the successful integration of three new high-performance computing nodes into its distributed research infrastructure. Located in Bologna, Helsinki, and Athens, the nodes collectively add over 2.4 petaflops of processing capacity to the existing network.
Faster Processing for Complex Datasets
Researchers working with multi-decade seismological archives have reported significant improvements in data processing times. Tasks that previously required 18 to 22 hours to complete are now finishing in under eight hours, reducing analysis cycles from monthly to weekly.
"This infrastructure milestone allows our scientific community to move from monthly to weekly analysis cycles." — Dr. Massimo Cocco, EPOS ERIC CEO
