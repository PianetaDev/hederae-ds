EPOS ERIC: New Supercomputing Nodes Accelerate Climate Modelling Research
The latest expansion of the European Plate Observing System brings three new computing hubs online, reducing processing time by up to 60% for seismological and climate datasets.
EF
Elena Ferretti
22 marzo 2026
6 min
The European Plate Observing System (EPOS ERIC) announced this week the successful integration of three new high-performance computing nodes into its distributed research infrastructure. Located in Bologna, Helsinki, and Athens, the nodes collectively add over 2.4 petaflops of processing capacity to the existing network.
Faster Processing for Complex Datasets
Researchers working with multi-decade seismological archives have reported significant improvements in data processing times. Tasks that previously required 18 to 22 hours to complete are now finishing in under eight hours, according to the EPOS technical team. This acceleration is particularly impactful for near-real-time monitoring of tectonic activity across the Euro-Mediterranean region.
"This infrastructure milestone allows our scientific community to move from monthly to weekly analysis cycles, which fundamentally changes how we respond to geological events." — Dr. Massimo Cocco, EPOS ERIC CEO
The new nodes are fully integrated with the existing EPOS ICS-C (Integrated Core Services — Central Hub) and are accessible to all member institutions through the EPOS Data Portal. Access protocols follow the FAIR data principles, ensuring full interoperability with existing workflows and third-party research platforms.
